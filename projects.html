Projects

The goal of this project was to build a file system simulator in C++. To accomplish this, my partner and I had to implement multiple Linux commands, while also figuring out a proper way to hash information. For my part, I was the one who primarirly designed the Linux commands: cd, pwd, ls, touch, cat, etc. My partner was in charge of creating the hash maps. Doing this project was a ton of fun, and I was extremely helpful in learning how to implement different data structures learned in class.

The aim of this project was to demostrate the real life applications of partial differential equations (PDE). For my group's project, we attempt the task of showing how the Black Scholes Equation, a PDE, was extremely useful in the realm of stock options. To do this, we showed how the equation was derived from known market facts, and how these terms, when connected together, realized an equation that was risk free. For my part in this project, I wrote the introduction, the derivation, and the conclusion.

For this project, my group and I attempt to show the applications of complex variables in the area of conformal mappings. Essentially, we showed how using a complex equation allows the user to view/solve their equation in a simpler manner. For my part, I showed how having an analytic function allows us to have a conformal mapping (a mapping that preserves the angle between two differential arcs).
